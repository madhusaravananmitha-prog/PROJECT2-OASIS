# Customer Segment Analysis

| Cluster | Customers | Characteristics                                  | Segment Name      |
| ------- | --------: | ------------------------------------------------ | ----------------- |
| 0       |       545 | Highest income, highest spending, high purchases | Premium Customers |
| 1       |       568 | Medium income, very low spending                 | Regular Customers |
| 2       |       484 | Lowest income, lowest spending                   | Budget Customers  |
| 3       |       608 | High income, moderate spending, active buyers    | Loyal Customers   |

---

## Segment 0 – Premium Customers

* Average Income: ₹76,123
* Average Spending: 1367.69
* Highest catalog and store purchases
* Most valuable customer segment

### Recommendation

* VIP rewards
* Exclusive product launches
* Personalized offers
* Premium membership programs

---

## Segment 1 – Regular Customers

* Average Income: ₹38,420
* Average Spending: 87.17
* Moderate purchasing activity

### Recommendation

* Product recommendations
* Bundle offers
* Seasonal promotions

---

## Segment 2 – Budget Customers

* Average Income: ₹30,431
* Average Spending: 90.59
* Lowest purchasing power

### Recommendation

* Discounts and coupons
* Affordable product packages
* Cashback campaigns

---

## Segment 3 – Loyal Customers

* Average Income: ₹58,863
* Average Spending: 661.42
* Frequent buyers
* Strong store and web purchase activity

### Recommendation

* Loyalty points program
* Retention campaigns
* Upselling and cross-selling

---

# Key Insights

### Insight 1

Premium Customers (Cluster 0) generate the highest revenue despite being only 545 customers.

### Insight 2

Loyal Customers (Cluster 3) form the largest segment with 608 customers and show consistent purchasing behavior.

### Insight 3

Budget Customers (Cluster 2) have the lowest income and spending levels, indicating strong price sensitivity.

### Insight 4

Regular Customers (Cluster 1) represent an opportunity for targeted marketing to increase spending.

### Insight 5

Income and spending show a positive relationship, making them strong variables for segmentation.

---

# Business Recommendations

1. Create VIP programs for Premium Customers.
2. Launch loyalty rewards for Loyal Customers.
3. Offer discounts and promotions for Budget Customers.
4. Use personalized marketing campaigns for Regular Customers.
5. Implement recommendation systems based on customer segments.

---

# Conclusion

Using K-Means clustering, customers were successfully grouped into four meaningful segments based on income, spending behavior, and purchasing patterns. These segments provide valuable insights for targeted marketing, customer retention strategies, and revenue growth initiatives.
